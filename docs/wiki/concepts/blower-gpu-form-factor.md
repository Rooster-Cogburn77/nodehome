# Blower GPU Form Factor

## What It Is
A blower-style GPU uses a single radial fan that draws air in and exhausts it entirely out the rear bracket of the card. This is in contrast to open-air coolers that use 2-3 axial fans to blow air across heatsink fins and into the case.

## Why It Matters for Dense Builds
- **Exactly 2 slots wide** - predictable, consistent sizing
- **Rear exhaust** - heat goes out of the chassis, not into neighboring cards
- **Mandatory for 3+ GPUs in 4U rack** - open-air cards at 2.7-3.5 slots physically cannot fit

## Thermal Tradeoff
Blower cards run hotter and louder than open-air equivalents under load. A single blower 3090 in a gaming PC is worse than an open-air card. But in a 3-GPU rack server, blower is the only option that doesn't create a thermal cascade.

## Market Availability (April 2026)
Blower 3090s are scarce compared to open-air. Most consumer 3090s were sold as gaming cards (open-air). Blower variants were primarily sold to server/workstation OEMs and bulk buyers. This creates the "blower premium" of 10-25% over equivalent open-air cards.

### Key Insight: eBay Is Not Their Primary Channel
These China-based sellers don't treat eBay as a real sales channel. They likely move volume through:
- WeChat / direct B2B relationships
- Alibaba (though we found Alibaba pricing is actually higher)
- Direct sales to Asian server builders
- Corporate/enterprise bulk orders

eBay is a storefront window, not the shop. The listings sit there for the occasional western buyer who finds them. That's why:
- Inventory doesn't move (same numbers for weeks)
- Prices don't drop despite zero sales
- Messages go unanswered
- Sellers would "rather have 40 cards sit on a shelf than drop the price by $100 and risk crashing their own market"

### The "Safety Floor" Phenomenon
High-volume sellers like MemoryPartner (40+ WinFast cards listed) set a price floor and wait. They know:
- A corporate buyer will eventually come along and buy 10-40 at once
- Dropping price for individual sales isn't worth the signaling effect
- Their cost basis is low enough to wait indefinitely

### What Made kuaka02 Different
Ada (kuaka02) treated the inquiry like a sales opportunity:
- Responded within hours
- Negotiated professionally
- Created a custom listing at the agreed price
- Provided shipping timeline and logistics
- Communicated like a salesperson, not a warehouse

### Tactical Lessons
1. **Go wide** - Message many sellers simultaneously. Most won't respond.
2. **Weekend timing** - Saturday messages into Shenzhen (Saturday night local) may help catch sellers reviewing their week.
3. **Bulk leverage is real** - "3 cards at once" gets attention from sellers who respond.
4. **Don't wait for one seller** - The frozen market means parallel negotiation is the only way to move fast.
5. **eBay auto-decline floors** - scwcomputers auto-declined $350 and $400 but accepted $420 on RAM. Sellers set hard floors in the offer system.
